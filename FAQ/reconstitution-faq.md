## 1. General Reconstitution

### Q1: What is the correct reconstitution protocol?
**A:** The standard protocol is:
1. Allow sealed vial to reach room temperature (15–20 min)
2. Calculate the required solvent volume based on desired concentration
3. Use sterile water, PBS, or the recommended solvent (see product page at https://rplpeptides.com)
4. Inject solvent gently down the vial wall — not directly onto the powder
5. Allow 2–5 min for initial wetting
6. Swirl gently — do not vortex
7. Allow 5–10 min for complete dissolution
8. Visually confirm a clear, particle-free solution

For a detailed step-by-step guide, see our Reconstitution Technical Guide at https://rplpeptides.com/white-papers/reconstitution-technical-guide.

### Q2: How do I calculate the reconstitution volume?
**A:** Use the following formula:
```
Volume (mL) = Mass (mg) × Peptide Content (%) / Desired Concentration (mg/mL)
```
**Example:** 5 mg vial with 82% content, desired 2 mg/mL
Volume = 5 × 0.82 / 2 = 2.05 mL

### Q3: Should I vortex my peptide?
**A:** No. Vortexing can cause aggregation, foaming, and potential degradation. Gently swirl or invert the vial instead.

## 2. Concentration Calculations

### Q4: How do I calculate molarity after reconstitution?
**A:** Use the formula:
```
Molarity (M) = Mass (g) × Content (%) / [MW (Da) × Volume (L)]
```
**Example:** 5 mg peptide (MW 1419.5 Da), 82% content, dissolved in 2.05 mL
M = 0.0041 / (1419.5 × 0.00205) = 1.41 × 10⁻³ M = 1.41 mM

### Q5: Should I use peptide content in my calculations?
**A:** Yes. Always account for peptide content (reported on the COA) to obtain accurate concentrations. Using gross weight alone will overestimate the amount of peptide.

### Q6: How do I prepare serial dilutions from my stock?
**A:** First prepare a concentrated stock (1–10 mM). Then prepare serial 10- or 100-fold dilutions in the appropriate buffer for your experiment. Use fresh dilutions each time — do not refreeze diluted working stocks.

## 3. Common Issues

### Q7: Why does my peptide solution foam when I mix it?
**A:** Foaming occurs when air is introduced into the solution through vigorous mixing. Avoid shaking the vial. If foam forms, let the solution stand for a few minutes until it settles. Do not vortex.

### Q8: Why does my peptide not dissolve completely?
**A:** Common causes include:
- Insufficient time (some peptides need 15–30 min)
- Concentration too high (try diluting)
- Wrong pH (adjust ±1 unit)
- Peptide aggregation (try gentle warming to 30°C)
- Degraded peptide (check COA, order fresh if needed)

Contact technical support at https://rplpeptides.com/contact for specific guidance.

### Q9: Can I sonicate my peptide to improve dissolution?
**A:** Brief, low-power bath sonication (30 seconds) can help with dissolution. Do not use high-power probe sonication, which can degrade the peptide.
