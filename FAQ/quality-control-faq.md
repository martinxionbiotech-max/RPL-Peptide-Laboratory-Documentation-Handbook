## 1. Quality Standards

### Q1: What quality control tests are performed on RPL Peptide products?
**A:** Each batch undergoes comprehensive QC testing including:
- **Appearance:** Visual inspection of lyophilized powder
- **Identity:** HPLC retention time match and ESI-TOF MS confirmation
- **Purity:** RP-HPLC at 214 nm (standard: ≥98%)
- **Peptide Content:** UV spectrophotometry or amino acid analysis
- **Counter-ion Content:** Ion chromatography (TFA content)
- **Water Content:** Karl Fischer titration (<5%)
- **Endotoxin:** LAL method (available upon request)

All test results are reported on the Certificate of Analysis, downloadable at https://rplpeptides.com.

### Q2: What purity grades does RPL Peptide offer?
**A:** We offer two standard purity grades:
- **Standard grade:** ≥98% (most products)
- **High purity grade:** ≥99% (available on request)

Custom purity specifications are available for bulk orders. Contact us at https://rplpeptides.com/contact.

### Q3: How is peptide purity defined?
**A:** Purity is defined as the area percent of the main peak on analytical RP-HPLC at 214 nm UV detection. This represents the relative purity of the peptide compared to other UV-absorbing compounds in the sample. For a comprehensive understanding of purity definitions and measurement, see our white paper at https://rplpeptides.com/white-papers/understanding-peptide-purity.

### Q4: What is the difference between purity and peptide content?
**A:** **Purity** (by HPLC area %) measures chemical homogeneity. **Peptide content** (by UV/AAA) measures the actual peptide mass as a percentage of total weight. Both are needed for full characterization. A peptide can be 99% pure but only 80% content (due to counter-ions and moisture).

## 2. Analytical Methods

### Q5: Why do you use 214 nm for HPLC detection?
**A:** The peptide bond absorbs strongly at ~214 nm, providing universal, sequence-independent detection. Detection at 280 nm (aromatic amino acids) would not detect all peptide species.

### Q6: What is the typical HPLC column and method used?
**A:** We use C18 reversed-phase columns (5 μm, 4.6 × 250 mm) with water-acetonitrile gradients containing 0.1% TFA, flow rate 1.0 mL/min, detection at 214 nm. Full method details are available at https://rplpeptides.com/white-papers/how-to-read-hplc.

### Q7: Do you test for residual solvents?
**A:** Yes. Residual solvent testing (by GC headspace analysis) is performed as needed, particularly for products where organic solvents are used during synthesis or purification.

## 3. Batch-to-Batch Consistency

### Q8: How consistent are batches from RPL Peptide?
**A:** Our validated manufacturing processes ensure high batch-to-batch consistency. Typical batch-to-batch variation in HPLC purity is <1%. Major variations would trigger a process investigation.

### Q9: What happens if a batch does not meet specifications?
**A:** Any batch that fails to meet specifications is quarantined, investigated, and either re-processed or rejected. Non-conforming batches are never released for distribution.
