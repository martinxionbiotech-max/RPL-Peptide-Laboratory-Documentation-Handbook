## 1. General Information

### Q1: What is a Technical Data Sheet (TDS)?
**A:** A Technical Data Sheet (TDS) is a document that summarizes the physical and chemical properties of a peptide product, including molecular formula, molecular weight, CAS number, appearance, solubility, storage recommendations, and safety information. It is designed for quick reference during experimental planning. All TDS documents are available at https://rplpeptides.com.

### Q2: What information does a typical RPL Peptide TDS include?
**A:** A complete TDS includes:
- Product name and synonyms
- CAS number
- Molecular formula and weight
- Sequence (for peptides)
- Appearance
- Purity specification
- Reconstitution recommendations
- Storage conditions
- Stability information
- Safety and handling precautions
- Disclaimer and intended use statement

### Q3: How is a TDS different from a COA?
**A:** A TDS is a specification sheet that describes the product's intended properties and standard quality parameters. A Certificate of Analysis (COA) provides the **actual measured values** for a specific batch. The TDS tells you what to expect; the COA tells you what was measured. Both are important for complete product documentation. Learn more in our COA FAQ at https://rplpeptides.com/knowledge-base/coa-faq.

### Q4: Where can I find TDS documents for RPL Peptide products?
**A:** TDS documents are available for each product on the product page at https://rplpeptides.com. They can also be provided upon request through our support channels.

## 2. Technical Specifications

### Q5: What does the CAS number tell me?
**A:** The CAS (Chemical Abstracts Service) Registry Number is a unique numerical identifier for every chemical substance. It provides a precise, unambiguous way to identify a compound regardless of naming conventions. You can use it to search for published research, regulatory information, and safety data.

### Q6: How is molecular weight reported on the TDS?
**A:** Molecular weight (MW) is reported in Daltons (Da) as the monoisotopic mass of the peptide free base (without counter-ion). The actual MW of the salt form will differ due to the counter-ion mass. For research requiring precise molarity calculations, account for the counter-ion contribution.

### Q7: Why do you report appearance if all peptides look similar?
**A:** Appearance is a simple but valuable quality indicator. Most peptides are white to off-white lyophilized powders. Any significant deviation from the described appearance (discoloration, visible moisture, unusual texture) may indicate handling or stability issues.

## 3. Data Interpretation

### Q8: What does "peptide content" mean on a TDS?
**A:** Peptide content is the percentage of the total mass that is the actual peptide, excluding counter-ions, residual moisture, and any residual solvents. It is determined by amino acid analysis or UV spectrophotometry. This value is essential for accurate concentration calculations.

### Q9: How do I calculate the amount of peptide for my experiment?
**A:**
Effective peptide mass = Gross weight × Peptide content (%)

**Example:**
- Vial label: 5 mg gross weight
- Peptide content: 82%
- Effective peptide mass: 5 × 0.82 = 4.1 mg

Use effective peptide mass for all reconstitution and dosing calculations.

---

## 4. Special Cases

### Q10: What does "TFA salt" mean on a TDS?
**A:** TFA (trifluoroacetic acid) salt indicates that the peptide is supplied as a trifluoroacetate salt, which is the standard form following HPLC purification using TFA-containing mobile phases. The TFA counter-ion contributes to the total vial weight. Refer to our counter-ion guide at https://rplpeptides.com/white-papers/counter-ion-guide for detailed information.

### Q11: What if a peptide has multiple CAS numbers?
**A:** Some peptides may have multiple CAS numbers corresponding to different salt forms, sequences, or stereoisomers. The TDS will specify which CAS number applies to the product as supplied. Always reference the product-specific CAS number when searching literature.
