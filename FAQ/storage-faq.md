## 1. General Storage

### Q1: How should I store lyophilized peptides?
**A:** Lyophilized peptides should be stored at -20°C in their original sealed vials, protected from light and moisture. Under these conditions, most peptides remain stable for 2+ years. Detailed storage recommendations for each product are listed on the product page at https://rplpeptides.com and in the accompanying Technical Data Sheet.

### Q2: Can I store peptides at 4°C instead of -20°C?
**A:** Yes, for short-term storage (up to 12 months) 4°C is acceptable, but degradation will occur faster than at -20°C. For long-term storage, -20°C is strongly recommended. For a complete discussion of storage conditions and their impact on stability, refer to our Storage and Handling white paper at https://rplpeptides.com/white-papers/peptide-storage-and-handling.

### Q3: Do I need to protect peptides from light?
**A:** Yes. UV and fluorescent light can cause photo-oxidation of susceptible amino acids (especially Trp, Tyr, and Met). Store peptides in their original packaging or in opaque containers. Avoid prolonged exposure to laboratory lighting.

### Q4: Should I use a frost-free freezer?
**A:** No. Frost-free freezers undergo temperature cycling during defrost cycles, which can expose peptides to temperatures above 0°C. Use a manual-defrost freezer for stable temperature maintenance.

## 2. Opening and Handling

### Q5: Should I let the vial warm up before opening?
**A:** Yes. Always allow the sealed vial to reach room temperature (15–25°C) before opening. This takes approximately 15–20 minutes. Opening a cold vial causes moisture condensation inside, which can degrade the lyophilized peptide.

### Q6: What is the best practice for opening the vial?
**A:** After temperature equilibration, carefully remove the flip-top cap and rubber stopper. Use sterile technique. Work quickly to minimize exposure to ambient humidity. After use, reseal if storing the remaining powder.

### Q7: How do I protect the peptide from moisture during storage?
**A:** Store vials in a sealed container with desiccant (silica gel packets). For frequently accessed vials, consider storing in a desiccator cabinet. Minimize the number of times a vial is opened.

---

## 3. After Reconstitution

### Q8: How long can reconstituted peptide be stored?
**A:** At 4°C: typically up to 30 days. At -20°C (aliquoted): up to 6 months. Always check the specific stability data for your peptide. See our Stability FAQ at https://rplpeptides.com/knowledge-base/stability-faq for detailed information.

### Q9: Should I aliquot reconstituted peptide?
**A:** Yes. Always aliquot reconstituted peptide into single-use portions before freezing. Repeated freeze-thaw cycles cause degradation and should be avoided. For best results, flash-freeze aliquots in liquid nitrogen before transferring to -20°C storage.

### Q10: What is the best tube for storing reconstituted peptide?
**A:** Use polypropylene microcentrifuge tubes for standard storage. For very dilute solutions (<100 μM), use low-binding (LoBind) tubes to minimize adsorption. Avoid glass tubes for long-term storage of aqueous solutions.
