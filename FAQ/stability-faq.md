## 1. General Stability

### Q1: What factors affect peptide stability?
**A:** The main factors affecting peptide stability are:
- **Temperature:** Higher temperature accelerates all degradation pathways
- **pH:** Most peptides are most stable at pH 4–6
- **Moisture:** Lyophilized peptides are hygroscopic — moisture accelerates degradation
- **Light:** UV and visible light can cause photo-oxidation
- **Oxygen:** Air exposure leads to oxidation of susceptible residues
- **Microorganisms:** Contamination can rapidly degrade peptides in solution

A detailed discussion of all degradation pathways is available in our Stability and Degradation white paper at https://rplpeptides.com/white-papers/peptide-stability-and-degradation.

### Q2: What is the typical shelf life of a lyophilized peptide?
**A:** Under proper storage (-20°C, protected from light and moisture), most RPL Peptide products have a shelf life of 2–5 years. The retest date on the COA provides a conservative estimate. Many peptides remain stable well beyond this date when stored properly.

### Q3: How fast do peptides degrade at room temperature?
**A:** Degradation rates vary by peptide sequence. In lyophilized form, degradation at 25°C is typically 100–500 times faster than at -20°C. Short-term exposure (hours to days) during handling is generally acceptable, but extended storage at room temperature is not recommended.

### Q4: What is the most common cause of peptide degradation?
**A:** For lyophilized peptides: moisture absorption during handling. For reconstituted peptides: hydrolysis and deamidation. Proper handling and storage practices are the most effective ways to prevent degradation.

## 2. Signs of Degradation

### Q5: How do I know if my peptide has degraded?
**A:** Signs of degradation include:
- Reduced solubility or cloudy solution after reconstitution
- Yellowing or discoloration of the lyophilized powder
- New peaks on HPLC compared to the original COA
- Loss of biological activity
- Unusual smell (from microbial contamination)

### Q6: What does yellowing of the powder mean?
**A:** Yellowing typically indicates oxidation or thermal degradation. The peptide may have been exposed to heat, oxygen, or light. A yellow peptide should be re-analyzed before use.

### Q7: Can I still use a peptide past its retest date?
**A:** We recommend re-analyzing the peptide (at minimum by HPLC) before use. If purity is still within specifications, it may be acceptable. If purity has decreased below 98%, we recommend ordering a fresh batch.

## 3. Stability Testing

### Q8: Does RPL Peptide conduct stability studies?
**A:** Yes. We perform both real-time (long-term) and accelerated stability studies on our products. Stability data is available upon request through https://rplpeptides.com/contact.

### Q9: What is accelerated stability testing?
**A:** Accelerated stability studies use elevated temperature (25–40°C) to predict degradation rates at recommended storage conditions. The Arrhenius equation is used to extrapolate results. For example, 1 month at 40°C may approximate 6 months at 4°C.

### Q10: Can you provide stability data specific to my experimental conditions?
**A:** Yes. Contact us at https://rplpeptides.com/contact with your specific conditions (temperature, buffer pH, concentration), and we will provide the best available stability information.
