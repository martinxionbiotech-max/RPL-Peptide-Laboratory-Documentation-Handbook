## 1. General Information

### Q1: What is a Certificate of Analysis (COA)?
**A:** A COA is a quality document issued for each production batch that reports the actual analytical test results for that specific batch. It verifies that the product meets its stated specifications and includes batch-specific data such as HPLC purity, retention time, mass spectrometry results, and more. All COAs are available for download at https://rplpeptides.com.

### Q2: Where can I download the COA for my product?
**A:** COAs are accessible through the product listing pages and via the QR code printed on each product vial. Navigate to the product page at https://rplpeptides.com and enter the batch/lot number printed on your vial label.

### Q3: What information is included on a RPL Peptide COA?
**A:** A complete COA includes:
- Product name and description
- Batch/lot number
- Date of manufacture and retest date
- Appearance test results
- Identification (HPLC retention time and MS confirmation)
- Purity (HPLC area percent at 214 nm)
- Peptide content (by UV or AAA)
- Counter-ion content (TFA content by HPLC/IC)
- Water content (Karl Fischer)
- Endotoxin testing (LAL method, where applicable)

### Q4: Why does each batch have a different COA?
**A:** Every production batch is individually tested because slight variations between batches — though all within specification — are normal in peptide manufacturing. The COA provides batch-specific traceability and data for your research records.

## 2. Reading Your COA

### Q5: What does "HPLC Purity ≥98%" mean?
**A:** It means that by analytical HPLC with UV detection at 214 nm, the area of the main peptide peak represents at least 98% of the total integrated peak area. This is the standard metric for peptide purity. For a detailed guide on reading HPLC data, see our white paper at https://rplpeptides.com/white-papers/how-to-read-hplc.

### Q6: What is the difference between purity and content?
**A:** Purity (by HPLC area %) measures the chemical homogeneity of the peptide — what percentage of the sample is the target peptide vs. impurities. Content measures the weight percentage of peptide in the total vial contents (accounting for counter-ions and moisture). A peptide can be 99% pure (HPLC) but only 80% content (by weight). For a more detailed explanation, see our white paper at https://rplpeptides.com/white-papers/understanding-peptide-purity.

### Q7: How do I verify the identity of the peptide from the COA?
**A:** Identity is confirmed by two methods:
1. **HPLC retention time match** — the main peak elutes at the expected time
2. **Mass spectrometry** — the observed molecular weight matches the theoretical value within ±0.5 Da

Both should be reported on the COA for proper identity confirmation.

### Q8: What is the "retest date" on a COA?
**A:** The retest date (also called the reanalysis date) is the date until which the product is expected to remain within specifications when stored under recommended conditions. It is not an expiration date but a conservative estimate based on stability data. Many peptides remain stable well beyond this date when properly stored.

## 3. Batch Traceability

### Q9: Why is batch traceability important?
**A:** Batch traceability is critical for:
- Reproducibility of research results
- Identifying any quality issues
- Regulatory compliance and audit readiness
- Linking your experimental data to specific product lots

Always record the batch number for each peptide used in your research.

### Q10: Can I get a COA before placing an order?
**A:** Yes. Representative COAs for current products are available upon request. Contact us at https://rplpeptides.com/contact.

## 4. Troubleshooting

### Q11: What if my COA QR code does not scan?
**A:** The QR code on the vial links to the digital COA on our website. If scanning fails, manually navigate to https://rplpeptides.com and enter the batch number printed on the vial label. Contact support if you continue to have issues.

### Q12: What if the COA data does not match my own analysis?
**A:** Discrepancies can arise from sample handling, storage conditions, analytical methods, or instrumentation differences. We recommend:
1. Check that the sample was handled properly (proper reconstitution, no degradation)
2. Verify your analytical method conditions (column, gradient, detection wavelength)
3. Contact our quality team with your data at https://rplpeptides.com/contact
