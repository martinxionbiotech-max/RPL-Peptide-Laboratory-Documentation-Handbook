## 1. Vial Types

### Q1: What type of vials are used for RPL Peptide products?
**A:** Our standard packaging is Type I borosilicate glass vials with rubber stoppers and aluminum flip-off seals. This pharmaceutical-grade packaging ensures chemical inertness and maintains product integrity. Detailed specifications are available at https://rplpeptides.com.

### Q2: What sizes of vials are available?
**A:** Standard vial sizes range from 2 mL to 20 mL, depending on the product quantity. Typical configurations:
- 1–5 mg: 2 mL vial
- 10–50 mg: 5 mL vial
- 100–500 mg: 10 mL vial
- 1 g: 20 mL vial

### Q3: Are the vials sterile?
**A:** Vials are supplied sterile and sealed under clean conditions. The rubber stopper maintains the sterility seal until the vial is opened. For aseptic applications, work in a laminar flow hood.

### Q4: Why are amber vials used for some products?
**A:** Amber vials provide protection from UV and visible light, which can degrade light-sensitive peptides. Products containing susceptible amino acids (Trp, Tyr, Met) are packaged in amber glass.

## 2. Vial Label

### Q5: What information is on the vial label?
**A:** Each vial label includes:
- Product name
- Batch/lot number
- Quantity (mg per vial)
- Storage conditions
- Date of manufacture
- QR code linking to the COA
- Safety warnings
- Company name and website: https://rplpeptides.com

### Q6: Can you provide custom labeling?
**A:** Custom labeling is available for bulk and contract manufacturing orders. Contact us at https://rplpeptides.com/contact to discuss requirements.

---

## 3. Secondary Packaging

### Q7: How are vials packaged for shipment?
**A:** Vials are packed in foam-insert or vacuum-formed trays inside sturdy cardboard boxes. Temperature-sensitive shipments include gel packs or dry ice with insulated containers. See our Shipping FAQ at https://rplpeptides.com/knowledge-base/shipping-faq for details.

### Q8: Is desiccant included in the packaging?
**A:** Yes. Desiccant packets (silica gel) are included in the secondary packaging to maintain low humidity during storage and transport.
