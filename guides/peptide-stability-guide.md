## 1. Introduction

Stability is the ability of a peptide to maintain its chemical and physical properties over time. This guide covers factors affecting stability and best practices for maintaining peptide integrity.

## 2. Factors Affecting Stability

Temperature: Higher temperature accelerates all degradation pathways.
- pH: Most peptides are most stable at pH 4–6.
- Moisture: Lyophilized peptides are hygroscopic.
- Light: UV and visible light cause photo-oxidation.
- Oxygen: Air exposure leads to oxidation.

## 3. Dry Powder Stability

Lyophilized peptides stored at -20°C typically remain stable for 2–5 years. Degradation rate is approximately 0.5–1% per year under optimal conditions. Lower temperatures (-80°C) can extend stability beyond 5 years.

## 4. Solution Stability

| Condition | Duration |
|:----------|:--------:|
| 4°C | 30 days |
| -20°C (aliquoted) | 6 months |
| 25°C | Not recommended |
Reconstituted peptides degrade significantly faster than lyophilized forms.

## 5. Temperature Effects

| Temperature | Relative Degradation |
|:-----------|:--------------------:|
| -80°C | 1× (baseline) |
| -20°C | 2–5× |
| 4°C | 10–50× |
| 25°C | 100–500× |
| 37°C | 1000–5000× |

## 6. pH Effects

Most peptides are most stable at pH 4–6. At extremes: pH <3 → acid hydrolysis. pH >8 → base-catalyzed deamidation and racemization. Always buffer reconstitution solutions near neutral pH unless otherwise specified.

## 7. Oxidation

Susceptible residues:
- Methionine (Met) → Methionine sulfoxide
- Cysteine (Cys) → Cysteic acid
- Tryptophan (Trp) → Kynurenine
- Histidine (His) → 2-oxo-histidine
Minimize by: inert gas blanketing, antioxidant excipients, light protection.

## 8. Moisture

Lyophilized peptides absorb moisture rapidly when exposed to air. Moisture accelerates degradation by hydrolysis and provides a medium for microbial growth. Desiccants and minimal air exposure are essential.

## 9. Shelf Life

| Form | Shelf Life |
|:-----|:----------|
| Lyophilized, -20°C | 2–5 years |
| Lyophilized, 4°C | 12 months |
| Reconstituted, 4°C | 30 days |
| Reconstituted, -20°C | 6 months |

## 10. Best Practices

[ ] Store at -20°C in original sealed vials
[ ] Minimize freeze-thaw cycles (<3)
[ ] Use desiccated containers
[ ] Protect from light
[ ] Record storage start dates

## 11. Troubleshooting

Q: Why did my peptide purity decrease?
A: Check storage temperature, moisture exposure, and freeze-thaw history.

Q: Why is my solution cloudy?
A: Protein aggregation or precipitation. May indicate degradation.
