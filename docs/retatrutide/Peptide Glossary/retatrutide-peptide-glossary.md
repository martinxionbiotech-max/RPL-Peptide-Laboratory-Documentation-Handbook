# Peptide Glossary — Reference Terms

## A–C
**Amino Acid** — Building block of peptides; 20 standard types.
**cAMP** — Second messenger produced by adenylyl cyclase; key readout in GPCR assays.
**CAS Number** — Unique numerical identifier for chemical substances.
**COA (Certificate of Analysis)** — Quality document summarizing batch analytical results.

## D–G
**DPP-4** — Enzyme that degrades incretin peptides; resistance improves stability.
**GCGR (Glucagon Receptor)** — Receptor for glucagon; involved in glucose homeostasis and energy expenditure.
**GIPR** — Receptor for glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide.
**GLP-1R** — Receptor for glucagon-like peptide-1; key incretin target.
**GPCR** — G protein-coupled receptor family.

## H–R
**HPLC** — High-performance liquid chromatography for purity analysis.
**Incretin** — Gut hormone that potentiates insulin secretion.
**LC-MS** — Liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry for identity confirmation.
**Lyophilization** — Freeze-drying process; produces stable peptide cake.
**MOQ** — Minimum order quantity.

## S–Z
**SPPS** — Solid-phase peptide synthesis.
**TFA** — Trifluoroacetic acid; residual counterion from HPLC purification.
**Triple Agonist** — Peptide activating three receptor targets simultaneously.
**UPLC** — Ultra-performance liquid chromatography.
