
## Chapter 01: Product Overview

## 1.1 Introduction

MOTS-C (Mitochondrial Open Reading Frame of the Twelve S rRNA Type-C) is a 16-amino-acid mitochondrial-derived peptide (MDP) encoded by the mitochondrial 12S rRNA gene. It belongs to the family of mitochondrial-encoded peptides that mediate inter-organellar communication and regulate metabolic homeostasis. MOTS-C has garnered significant research interest for its roles in metabolic regulation, exercise physiology, insulin sensitivity, and age-related metabolic decline.

Unlike nuclear-encoded peptides, MOTS-C is transcribed and translated within the mitochondria, a unique biosynthetic origin that positions it as a mitochondrial signaling molecule capable of translocating to the nucleus under cellular stress conditions (e.g., glucose restriction, AMPK activation) to regulate gene expression.
