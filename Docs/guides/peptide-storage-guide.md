## 1. Introduction

Proper storage is the most critical factor in maintaining peptide quality. Even the highest-purity peptide will degrade rapidly under improper conditions. This guide provides comprehensive storage protocols for all RPL Peptide products.

## 2. Why Proper Storage Matters

- Prevents hydrolysis, oxidation, and deamidation
- Maintains solubility and reconstitution properties
- Ensures consistent experimental results
- Extends usable product life
- Protects research investment

## 3. Recommended Storage Temperatures

| Form | Temperature | Duration |
|:-----|:-----------:|:--------:|
| Lyophilized (long-term) | -20°C | 2+ years |
| Lyophilized (short-term) | 4°C | 12 months |
| Reconstituted (stock) | -20°C | 6 months (aliquoted) |
| Reconstituted (working) | 4°C | 30 days |

## 4. Short-Term Storage

For peptides used within 30 days:
- Store lyophilized vials at 4°C
- Keep in sealed container with desiccant
- Protect from light
- Minimize vial openings

## 5. Long-Term Storage

For peptides stored beyond 30 days:
- Transfer to -20°C freezer (manual defrost type)
- Use desiccated storage container
- Wrap vials in aluminum foil for light protection
- Record storage location and date

## 6. Freeze–Thaw Cycles

| Cycles | Impact | Recommendation |
|:------:|:------|:---------------|
| 1 | Minimal | Acceptable |
| 2–3 | Some degradation | Acceptable with caution |
| >3 | Significant | Not recommended |

Always aliquot reconstituted peptides into single-use portions before freezing.

## 7. Moisture Protection

- Allow sealed vials to reach room temperature before opening
- Use desiccant packets in storage containers
- Minimize time vials are open
- Store in low-humidity environment

## 8. Light Protection

| Light Type | Risk | Protection |
|:-----------|:----:|:-----------|
| Direct sunlight | Severe | Opaque container |
| Fluorescent | Moderate | Amber vials or foil wrap |
| UV | Severe | UV-blocking storage |
| LED | Low | Standard storage OK |

## 9. Transportation Considerations

- Use insulated container with gel packs
- Minimize transit time
- Include temperature indicator
- Inspect immediately upon receipt

## 10. Storage Best Practices

- [ ] Use manual-defrost freezer (not frost-free)
- [ ] Store peptides in original sealed vials
- [ ] Maintain storage log with dates
- [ ] Monitor freezer temperature regularly
- [ ] Keep backup generator or UPS for freezers
- [ ] Label all containers clearly

## 11. Common Storage Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Solution |
|:--------|:------------|:---------|
| Frost-free freezer | Temperature cycling | Use manual-defrost |
| Opening cold vial | Moisture condensation | Warm to room temp first |
| No desiccant | Moisture absorption | Add silica gel packets |
| Repeated freeze-thaw | Degradation | Always aliquot |
| Light exposure | Photo-oxidation | Use amber/foil wrap |

## 12. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I store peptides at room temperature?**
A: Short-term (days) in lyophilized form only. Not recommended for extended periods.

**Q: How do I know if my freezer is suitable?**
A: Monitor temperature with a data logger. Fluctuations should be <3°C.

**Q: Should I store different peptides together?**
A: Yes, in well-sealed vials. Ensure labels are clearly visible.
