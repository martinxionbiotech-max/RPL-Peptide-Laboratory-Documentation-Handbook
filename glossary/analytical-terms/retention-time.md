# Retention Time

## Definition

Retention time (RT) is the time elapsed between sample injection and the maximum peak response of a compound as it elutes from an HPLC column. It is a characteristic parameter used for compound identification.

## Overview

Retention time is the primary parameter for identifying compounds in HPLC analysis. Under consistent chromatographic conditions (column, mobile phase, temperature, gradient), each compound has a characteristic retention time that aids in identification.

## Technical Explanation

**Factors Affecting Retention Time:**
- Column chemistry (C18, C8, C4 — affects hydrophobicity-based retention)
- Mobile phase composition (organic solvent percentage, pH)
- Gradient profile (steep vs. shallow)
- Column temperature
- Flow rate
- Peptide properties (hydrophobicity, charge, size)

**Relative Retention Time (RRT):**
RRT = RT of impurity peak / RT of main peak
RRT is used to identify known impurities regardless of absolute retention time shifts between runs.

## Importance in Peptide Documentation

Retention time data on chromatograms allows researchers and QC analysts to:
- Verify consistency between batches
- Identify known impurities by RRT
- Compare results with reference standards
- Confirm method reproducibility

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can retention time alone confirm peptide identity?**
A: No. Retention time is supportive evidence but not conclusive. Different peptides can have similar retention times. Mass spectrometry is required for definitive identity confirmation.

**Q: Why might retention time shift between analyses?**
A: Common causes: column aging, temperature fluctuation, mobile phase preparation variation, instrument differences, or sample overloading.
